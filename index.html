About Domain Extensions

Domain extensions (also known as top-level domains) are the last part of an internet domain, such as .com, .cn, .org. They are generally divided into different types, including country code top-level domains (ccTLD), generic top-level domains (gTLD), and new generic top-level domains (new gTLD).

Country Code Domains

Typically consist of two letters, representing specific countries or regions, such as .cn (China), .uk (United Kingdom), .jp (Japan).

Generic Top-Level Domains

Not specific to any country or region, such as .com (commercial), .org (organization), .net (network).
